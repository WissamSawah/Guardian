ABOUT US

At Guardian, we're building the world's safest worry-free transportation network for students and Special Ed, serving districts and parents alike.

OUR STORY

Guardian was formed in February of 2019. Although a new company, we’ve been working in different sectors of this industry for over 10 years. Our focus and drive for Guardian was simple, a safe worry-free experience for both parents and schools, lead with the focus of Technology and Security at heart.

GUARDIAN’S MODEL FOR SPECIAL NEEDS TRANSPORTATION.

Guardian supplements districts’ transportation needs using a variety of different accommodations, including SUVs, wheelchair accessible vans, and sedans. These solutions are there to assist in transporting McKinney-Vento, ESE/special needs, out-of-district students, hard-to-serve and multi-district trips; no job is too small for us!

Augmenting bus service with smaller capacity vehicles means that districts and schools no longer have to pay for unused capacity. This gives you the flexibility to expand and contract our fleet to accommodate the ever-changing requirements of these student populations. Guardian accomplishes all this, while reducing the cost of transportation programs by up to 20-30% on average.

WHAT SEPERATES US?

Saving

We know that in today’s economy and environment, every dollar makes a difference – education funds are no different. Guardian can save school districts 20-30% on average while also providing exceptional attention to service and quality.

We can promise you that no matter what we are facing or what other factors may arise, we will never compromise the safety of the Students while keeping the best interests of the districts in mind.

SERVICES

Out of District Transportation

Not all situations fit into a cookie-cutter approach, just like the out of district situations. With Guardian, we can ensure these transportation requirements are not only met, but also managed with quality and attention for students, with cost in mind.

Special Needs Students

We understand that when working with students with special needs not only needs attention and focus, but a personal perspective as well.
